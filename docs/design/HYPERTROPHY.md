The planning principles for a hypertrophy-focused workout (bodybuilding) center on maximizing the **Volume** (total sets) and ensuring adequate **Intensity of Effort** (proximity to failure) across a variety of exercises to maximize muscle growth.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the planning principles for hypertrophy focus:

## Hypertrophy Focus (Bodybuilding) Planning Principles

The primary goal is increasing muscle mass (hypertrophy), which relies mainly on mechanical tension derived from sufficient volume and effort.

| Principle | Detail | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Goal** | **Maximize Muscle Growth (Hypertrophy)**, primarily driven by **volume accumulation** and mechanical tension. | |
| **Volume (Total Sets)** | Maintain **10–20 sets per muscle group/movement pattern per week**. Volume increases gradually over the lifting career as needed for continued progression. | |
| **Frequency** | Each muscle group/movement pattern should be trained **2 or more times per week** to efficiently distribute volume and maximize gains. | |
| **Intensity (Load Distribution)** | **⅔–¾ of the volume** should be performed in the **6–12 rep range** (moderate loads, optimal for volume accumulation). | |
| **Lighter/Heavier Sets** | The remaining **¼–⅓ of volume** is split between the **1–6 rep range** (heavier loads, supporting progressive overload/strength) and the **12–20 rep range** (lighter loads, supporting joint health/variety). | |
| **Intensity of Effort (RPE)** | Sets must be adequately challenging (RPE 5+). The general working range is **5–10 RPE**. Compound lifts often use RPE 6–9; isolation lifts may push to RPE 7–10. | |
| **Progression Model** | **Intermediate/Advanced:** Use **Wave Loading** (Linear Periodization) on compound movements (e.g., Week 1: 12 Reps @ BaseLoad; Week 2: 10 Reps @ BaseLoad + 5lbs; Week 3: 8 Reps @ BaseLoad + 10lbs). Isolation lifts use **Double Progression** (increase reps within a set range before increasing load). | |
| **Advanced Planning** | Utilize **Accumulation Blocks** (higher volume, 8–15 reps, RPE 6–8) to build work capacity and hypertrophy, followed by **Intensification Blocks** if supporting strength is necessary. | |
| **Exercise Selection** | Ensure variety for balanced development. Program **1–2 compound exercises** and **1–3 isolation exercises** for each major muscle group. | |
| **Exercise Order** | **Compound/Multi-joint lifts** should be performed **before Isolation/Single-joint lifts** for the same muscle group to maximize performance on the most complex and fatiguing movements. Weak points may be prioritized earlier if fatigue doesn't compromise subsequent compound lifts. | |
| **Rest Periods** | Rest periods generally range from **1.5–2 minutes for isolation/machine lifts** and **2.5–5 minutes for compound lifts**. The guiding principle is to **rest until ready** to perform the next set at optimal quality. | |
| **Tempo** | Use **Standard Tempo** (Controlled eccentric, forceful concentric). Avoid excessively slow tempos (e.g., >4s eccentric) as this can reduce total volume and impulse, compromising growth. | |

## Hypertrophy Exercise Selection Categories

For hypertrophy, exercise selection focuses on ensuring balanced muscular development across all major muscle groups and movement patterns. The training must include both compound exercises (for efficiency and load) and isolation exercises (to target specific muscle heads or weak points).

| Movement Pattern | Primary Muscle Groups | Rationale/Application | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Squat Pattern** (Barbell, Leg Press, Hack Squat) | **Quads, Glutes** | Highly efficient for lower body volume. Includes free weight (taxing) and machine options (less lumbar stress). | |
| **Hip Hinge** (Deadlift, RDL, Back Extension) | **Glutes, Hamstrings, Spinal Erectors** | Essential for developing the posterior chain and achieving uniform growth. RDLs or Good Mornings are preferred for high volume due to controlled eccentrics. | |
| **Horizontal Push** (Bench, DB Press, Flys) | **Chest, Anterior Delts, Triceps** | Core movement for upper body development. Variety in angles (flat/incline) and movements (flys) is encouraged for comprehensive pec development. | |
| **Vertical Push** (OHP, Machine Press) | **Anterior Delts, Triceps, Middle Delts** | Key for shoulder development. Middle delts contribute significantly to vertical pushing, reducing the need for excessive direct side delt work unless it is a weak point. | |
| **Vertical Pull** (Chins, Lat Pulldowns) | **Lats, Biceps, Rear Delts** | Vital for back width (Lats). Vertical movement generally ensures a full range of motion for elbow flexors (biceps). | |
| **Horizontal Pull** (Rows, Cable Rows) | **Lats, Scapular Retractors, Rear Delts** | Vital for back thickness and posture. Rear delts and middle delts are hit heavily here. | |
| **Isolation Exercises** (Curls, Raises, Extensions) | **Specific Target Muscle** | Used to ensure specific muscle groups (e.g., biceps, calves) receive sufficient volume, especially when they are potential **weak points** or receive inadequate stress from compound lifts alone. | |

Hypertrophy training, in essence, is about spreading adequate total volume (10–20 sets/week) across a strategic mix of compound and isolation movements, ensuring the majority of the sets are performed with high effort in the convenient 6–12 rep range, and progressing loads over time as strength allows.

***

**Analogy:** Hypertrophy training is like managing a diverse investment portfolio. You must allocate the bulk of your resources (volume and effort) to the reliable, high-growth mutual funds (compound lifts in the 6–12 rep range) because they offer the broadest returns. However, to ensure balanced growth and maximize potential, you must also invest smaller amounts in specialized, targeted stocks (isolation exercises) to address specific market gaps (weak points) and ensure your overall portfolio (physique) is maximized and proportional.
